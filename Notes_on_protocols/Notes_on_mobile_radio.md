## What happens during a phone conversation: wireless transmission and reception

‚ÄčLet's now look at a simple example of a wireless system that most of us ‚Äčuse every day that of a cell phone. ‚ÄčLet's say that you're trying to call your mom, which you should ‚Äčdo as often as possible and we'll try to see at least at a high level ‚Äčwhat happens when we greet our mom and how our words are transmitted ‚Äčto the other end using different blocks of a typical wireless system. ‚ÄčSo let's say that you have called your mom and the phone rang. ‚ÄčShe picked up the phone and you greeted her saying, hi, mom. ‚ÄčWhen you say those words at a high level, ‚Äčthat sound goes to the modulator inside your phone among other things. ‚ÄčAnd as we know, modulator has the job of impressing that information, ‚Äčwhich is our greeting here on to a carrier wave. ‚ÄčSo modulator impresses that information onto a carrier wave. 

‚ÄčBut the phone's internally work on a default frequency, ‚Äčwhich may be different from the frequency assigned to your ‚Äčphone by the network operator or the cellular provider. ‚ÄčSo your phone intrinsically converts that carrier wave into the specific frequency ‚Äčassigned to it by other network operator which has got upconversion. ‚ÄčAnd once the signal is at the desired frequency, ‚Äčit undergoes a power amplification, just like before you go for ‚Äča long hike or long run, you fuel up, the wireless signal before it goes ‚Äčthe long distance between two phones, it has to fuel up itself and ‚Äčthe power amplification is the process that does exactly that. ‚ÄčAnd once the signal has been converted to the specific carrier wave frequency and ‚Äčit has been amplified in power, it goes through the antenna. ‚ÄčAnd this is the point where the electrical signal which resides here ‚Äčis converted into a wireless or electromagnetic or radio frequency signal. ‚ÄčNow, that radio frequency signal travels over the air and ‚Äčwhen it travels over the air it faces certain impediments. ‚ÄčThe most important and most significant of which is called attenuation or ‚Äčpart loss or transmission loss.

‚ÄčSo the incoming signal is also amplified inside your mother's phone. ‚ÄčAnd because the transmitter phone had to upconvert in frequency the receiver's ‚Äčphone, we need to downconvert in frequency to convert that carrier away ‚Äčfrequency into the default frequency that your phone can understand. ‚ÄčAnd just like the transmitter's phone had to use the modulator functionality, ‚Äčthe receiver's phone will need to use the demoderator functionality in order ‚Äčto extract the information that has been impressed upon the incoming carrier wave. ‚ÄčAnd when your mother's phone is able to demodulate that information, that greeting ‚Äčis then converted into voice and that is the same greeting you sent her, hi, mom. ‚ÄčAnd this is how when you talk to someone over phone they get to ‚Äčhear your voice and you in turn get to hear their voice. ‚ÄčNow, a few disclaimers here and a few notes, this is a very high level or ‚Äča simplified description of a real life wireless communication system. ‚ÄčThere are many other blocks that are crucial from engineering standpoint that ‚Äčwe don't show here simply because we don't have the requisite background in order to ‚Äčunderstand their functionality and significance.
